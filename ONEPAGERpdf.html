Problem

Most agent stacks become fragile at scale. They struggle with queue contention, poor scheduling fairness, weak throughput, and no clean mechanism for temporal credit assignment. That makes them hard to use for production AI infrastructure, robotics, simulation, and RL systems.

Solution
  • 64 hierarchical queues with round-robin scheduling.
  • Priority aging to prevent starvation under load.
  • Per-agent ring buffers for TD-error credit assignment.
  • 1M+ agent context support, in-memory or Redis-backed.
  • CPU / GPU / LPU / ANY affinity routing.
  • Signed license delivery and source-code packaging.
